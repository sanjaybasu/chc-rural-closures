Dear Dr. Brennan and Editorial Team,

We are pleased to submit the attached manuscript, "Community Health Centers as Rural Safety-Net Absorbers: A Center-Level Event Study of Rural Hospital Closures, 2014–2024," for consideration as a **Brief Communication** in the JHCPU Special Supplement on Policy Impact and Innovations in Community Health Centers, sponsored by the National Association of Community Health Centers (NACHC).

The manuscript provides the first center-level operational event-study evidence on how rural community health centers responded to local hospital closures from 2014 through 2024. Using a panel of 1,642 health centers linked to the UNC Sheps Center rural hospital closure registry, and the heterogeneity-robust Callaway–Sant'Anna staggered difference-in-differences estimator, we find that exposed centers absorbed approximately 14% more patients without measurable payer-mix erosion or broad clinical-quality decline, and with modest improvement in hypertension control. A pediatric-immunization signal points to service-line strain that warrants targeted federal investment.

The work directly addresses the supplement's call for evidence on rural access and impacts of hospital closures, the financial and clinical effects of federal/state/local policy on CHCs, and the operational consequences of the Health Center Program's "policy quilt." It complements existing Geiger Gibson program work on CHC financing and the Medicaid unwinding by characterizing operational behavior under closure exposure.
